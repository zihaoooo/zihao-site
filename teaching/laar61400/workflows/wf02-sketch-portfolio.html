AssignmentA multi-page site reading deck: site photography assembled as a joiner, with drawn and annotated overlays, and a material texture palette — composed and laid out in InDesign. Read the site through what you photograph, what you draw over it, and what you find in maps and archives.

01 Gather Site Material
Photograph the site systematically — overall views, approach sequence, ground surfaces, edges, vegetation, structures, and material close-ups. Supplement with satellite imagery and historical maps.
  • Aim for 30–50 field photos covering three scales: wide, medium, and close-up. You will need all three.
  • Use Adobe Scan or Microsoft Lens for phone shots — they flatten perspective and reduce shadow
  • Satellite imagery: Google Earth Pro → File → Save → Save Image at the scale you need
  • Historical maps: Sanborn Fire Insurance Maps (NYPL Digital Collections), USGS Historical Topographic Map Explorer, or city archives
  • Organize before opening Photoshop: /field-photos, /satellite, /maps, /reference
02 Build the Site Joiner Photoshop
Reference David Hockney's joiners — multiple overlapping photographs assembled into a composite that shows a space from several perspectives simultaneously. Let the seams and edges show. The fragmentation is part of the drawing.
  • New document at your target output size. For a horizontal strip: 3300 × 1100 px minimum
  • Place each photo as a Smart Object (File → Place Embedded) — preserves quality, allows non-destructive transforms
  • Arrange photos with deliberate overlap — edges and repetition create spatial rhythm
  • Expand the canvas as needed: Image → Canvas Size
  • Group related photo layers Ctrl G to keep the Layers panel manageable
This is not a panorama stitcher — don't try to match edges seamlessly. Vary angles, scales, and crops between adjacent photos intentionally. The joins are the drawing.
  • Try a horizontal strip for a sequence (approach → entry → site → edge) and a grid for close-ups
  • Compress time: show the same wall from two different angles in adjacent frames
  • Scale with Ctrl T (Free Transform) → hold Shift to constrain proportions within Smart Objects
  • Use Multiply blending mode on overlapping photo layers to let both layers read through each other
03 Layer Maps and Satellite Photoshop
Place satellite or historical map images as layers within your joiner composition. Set blending modes so photograph and map read simultaneously — neither fully obscuring the other.
  • Multiply blending mode — darkens; good for overlaying dark linework maps onto photos
  • Screen blending mode — lightens; good for light-toned satellite over darker photos
  • Adjust layer Opacity to 30–60% — enough to read both layers at once
  • Use a layer mask to reveal the map only in specific areas: select layer → Add Layer Mask → paint black to hide, white to reveal
  • Georeferenced maps create a spatial argument when placed against field photos of the same location
04 Draw and Annotate Over Photos Photoshop
Analog over digital. Print a portion of your joiner. Draw directly on the print — trace edges, hatch surfaces, annotate conditions with pen or pencil. Rescan at 300 dpi. Place back in Photoshop as a new layer above the photos.
  • Set the scanned drawing layer's blending mode to Multiply — white paper disappears, only the marks remain visible over the photos below
  • Clip a Curves adjustment layer to the scan to blow out any residual paper tone
  • The analog feedback loop is the point: the hand drawing responds to and interprets what the photograph shows
  • You can draw over the drawing multiple times — rescan and re-layer as needed
Text as drawing. Reference Julie Bargmann's site collages — analytical text floated over imagery at multiple scales and weights. Text is not a caption here; it is part of the spatial and argumentative composition of the page.
  • Add Photoshop text layers directly over the joiner: T to place text anywhere on the canvas
  • Mix scales deliberately: a large bold analytical statement + fine-grain descriptive notes at different locations
  • Set text to Multiply over light areas, or use white text over dark zones for legibility
  • Text subjects: site conditions, material descriptions, process observations, measurements, open questions
05 Adjust Tones and Export Photoshop
Photos taken at different times and conditions will look inconsistent. Unify the tonal range before exporting. Then export the joiner and individual texture samples separately for InDesign.
  • Add a Curves or Hue/Saturation adjustment layer at the top of the stack (unclipped) to apply a global tonal shift across all layers
  • For finer control, clip individual adjustment layers to specific photo Smart Objects
  • Joiner export: File → Export → Export As → JPEG, quality 9–10, full resolution
  • Texture palette samples: crop and export each close-up as a separate JPEG — name by material and location (concrete-south-edge.jpg)
06 Set Up the InDesign Document InDesign
New multi-page document. Plan your pages before you build — at minimum a joiner spread and a texture palette page.
  • 11"×17" landscape · 0.125" bleed on all sides · facing pages off (single-sided deck)
  • Plan page count before building: (1) site joiner · (2) texture palette · (3+) analytical overlays, sketch drawings as needed
  • Set margins and column guides here — they become the skeleton for your parent page
07 Build the Parent Page InDesign
The parent page is the master template. Anything placed here repeats on every page it is applied to.
  • Open via Pages panel → A-Parent. Double-click to edit.
  • Add automatic page numbers: draw a text frame → Type → Insert Special Character → Markers → Current Page Number
  • Place repeating elements: course name, project title area, margin rules
  • Apply to pages by dragging the parent onto page thumbnails in the Pages panel
  • To override a parent element on a specific page: Ctrl Shift click the element
08 Set Up Paragraph Styles InDesign
Define styles for title, caption, and annotation text. Apply consistently — never format text manually.
  • Open Paragraph Styles panel F11 → New Style
  • Define at minimum: Title, Caption, Annotation. Set font, size, leading, and color in each.
  • Changes to a style update all instances across the document instantly
09 Compose the Joiner Page InDesign
Place the Photoshop export. The joiner page can run edge-to-edge (full bleed) or within margins — either way, it should feel like a drawing, not a photograph.
  • Ctrl D to place — extend the frame to the bleed marks for a full-bleed image
  • Fit content proportionally: Ctrl Alt E — or fill frame: Ctrl Alt Shift E
  • Click inside the frame with the Direct Selection tool A to reposition the image within its crop
Add analytical text over the image using your paragraph styles. Reinforce the multi-scale typographic hierarchy started in Photoshop.
  • Draw text frames anywhere on the page — float them over the image at different positions
  • Keep text on its own layer above the image layer: Layers panel → New Layer
  • Use the Align panel to position text frames relative to page or image edges precisely
  • Mix Annotation and Caption styles at different scales across the page
10 Texture Palette Page InDesign
A small-multiple grid of material and surface close-ups — a site texture inventory. Equal-sized frames, each captioned with material, location, and a brief condition note. Together they form a material portrait of the site.
  • Build the grid with Step and Repeat: Ctrl Alt U — set offset and repeat count to fill the page evenly
  • Or draw one frame → Ctrl C → Ctrl Alt V (Paste in Place) and nudge with arrow keys
  • Keep all frames the same size and spacing — the uniformity makes the material differences legible
  • Use Object Styles to apply consistent stroke or background to all cells at once
Place each texture JPEG into its frame, fit to fill, and caption below using your Caption paragraph style.
  • Select all frames, then Ctrl D to load multiple images at once — use arrow keys to cycle through and click each frame to place
  • Fill frame: Ctrl Alt Shift E — crops to fill, adjust crop with Direct Selection tool A
  • Caption format: material · location on site · one observation. No full sentences needed.
  • The texture palette is a site argument, not a decoration — choose close-ups that reveal something about the site's material conditions
11 Export PDF InDesign
Export the full deck as a single PDF. Include bleed and crop marks for print; use Interactive PDF for screen-first sharing.
  • Ctrl E → PDF (Print) for physical output — include bleed marks and crop marks
  • PDF (Interactive) for screen — smaller file size, no bleed needed
  • Use Ctrl Alt Shift P (Package) before sharing — collects all linked images and fonts into one folder
