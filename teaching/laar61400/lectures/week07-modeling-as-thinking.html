Part II

What we model — and why

Physical / scale

  • Good for: experience, material, spatial pattern
  • Weak for: measurement, computation
  • Reach when: “does it work as a space?”

Digital 3D / geometric

  • Good for: exact form, dimension, fit
  • Cost: slow; tempts over-building
  • Reach when: form is the question

Data / GIS

  • Good for: patterns across a territory
  • A field of values, not a surface
  • Same site, a different question
Fit the model to the question, not the tool you know.

The assignment · WF04

Modeling to think

A view plan + section can't

  • Plan flattens height
  • Section cuts one line
  • 3D: the continuous ground, whole

The operations are logics

  • Loft: blend between profiles
  • Sweep: a section along a path
  • Patch: fit through constraints

A curb is just a breakline

  • Breakline: where the surface stops being smooth
  • Smooth field → Patch
  • Hard stop → Loft a face
Choose the model for the question. What you leave out is the argument. Build only when it earns its cost.
